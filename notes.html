Working Notes

The Shadow Institution: observations, propositions and questions arising from the study of Potteries Thinkbelt, Dukeries Thinkbelt and related projects.

This page records an ongoing investigation into the concept of the Shadow Institution. It presents working notes, observations and questions rather than finished conclusions.
← Dukeries Thinkbelt

Origin

The term "Shadow Institution" emerged during a conversation between Norman Fellows and Cedric Price in December 1978.

NF: "I think it's already here."

CP: "What is?"

NF: "The shadow institution."

CP: [Non-verbal response signifying agreement.]

At the time the meaning of the phrase remained uncertain. The author's own recollection is that he had first referred to "it" as a manifestation but was dissatisfied with that description and attempted to clarify what he meant.

Subsequent investigations suggest that the phrase may provide a useful framework through which a number of projects by Cedric Price and Norman Fellows can be understood as related investigations rather than isolated episodes.

Observation

A recurring feature of several projects associated with Cedric Price and Norman Fellows is their apparent ability to operate across more than one territorial scale simultaneously.

Although physically situated within particular territories, their organisational logic, capabilities and consequences extend beyond those territories.

This observation has led to the working proposition that a defining characteristic of a shadow institution may be its ability to establish relationships across multiple territorial scales.

Rather than being confined to a single level of organisation, the shadow institution appears capable of connecting local, regional, national and global systems through the distribution of capabilities, resources and opportunities.
